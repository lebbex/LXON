Justification

Comments aren't a feature to use in publicly packaged LXON. They add prolixity, which goes against the entire scope of LXON.

Well structured data should always be readable without comments, same rule as with code, however we do understand the need for some to comment on their data when, for example, sharing it for debugging, or simply reminding themselves what something means or what it's supposed to look like.

In short, comments are something to avoid using, but are supported for the rare occurences you may want them.

Syntax

A comment is very straightforward to write. Simply begin a new line with#and the rest of the line in full will be skipped by the parser.

Important

You cannot write inline comments, that means comments on the same line with data. Some friendly parsers will understand and keep the comment as a comment, however, to simplify the workload of most parsers, the standard is to only allow full-line comments. Writing inline comments would go against this standard and will likely break on many parsers, including the officially released ones.
